Recent progress in the field of pretrained Large Language Models (LLM) made commonsense knowledge available "out of the box" for vast range of scenarios: content generation, customer services, voice assistants, etc. Release of GPT-3.5 (known as ChatGPT) opened prospectives for building highly contextualizable conversational agents, capable to hold a dialog and reflect about various situations as well as on behalf of different social roles (e.g. kitchen chef, software developer, etc.). Such an advancement is highly relevant for the robotics domain, where embodied agents have to take care about generating their cyber-physical behaviour in accordance to users' requests. Robotics behaviour planning comprizes of combination of manipulation and motion planning problems. In this paper we focus on manipulation planning only and exclude motion planning as from a planning pipeline. Manipulation planning on a real robot is has a wider scope than classical sequence planning task, since it get highly affected by of constantly changing geometric configurations and unexpected changes and uncertanties of a real world. As a result, manipulation planning mechanism is required to be highly adaptive to as feedbacks signals generated by perception and execution systems. Related Links

There's a lot of excellent work that was introduced around the same time as ours.

Progressive Encoding for Neural Optimization introduces an idea similar to our windowed position encoding for coarse-to-fine optimization.

D-NeRF and NR-NeRF both use deformation fields to model non-rigid scenes.

Some works model videos with a NeRF by directly modulating the density, such as Video-NeRF, NSFF, and DyNeRF

There are probably many more by the time you are reading this. Check out Frank Dellart's survey on recent NeRF papers, and Yen-Chen Lin's curated list of NeRF papers.
